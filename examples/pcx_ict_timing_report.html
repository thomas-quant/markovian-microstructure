Models Under Test

PCX Expansion Model

2-state Markov machine. Tracks whether the prior day closed above/below the previous day's high/low, then predicts the next day's directional expansion target. Filtered by abnormal wick detection (>40% of range).

71.2% filtered accuracy

ICT Daily Bias

5-state swing-point machine. Identifies bullish/bearish active states through swing high/low structure. When active, predicts the market will run the previous day's liquidity level.

63.8% bullish active / 61.4% bearish active
Independence

Pearson r = −0.045 (p = 0.29), Chi-squared p = 0.33, Cramer’s V = 0.04. Statistically Independent — the models are not measuring the same thing.

Current Findings Summary

1. Both models have independent predictive power

PCX (71.2% filtered) and ICT (63.8% bullish, 61.4% bearish) both beat baseline at statistically significant levels. They are not correlated (Cramer’s V = 0.04) — combining them is valid.

2. Combined signal reaches ~79% accuracy

When both agree on direction and the PCX wick filter passes, accuracy jumps to 79.0% long and 77.5% short. The cost: only ~37 qualifying days per year on average.

3. Targets resolve overnight, not in NY

Median hit time is 4:30 AM ET. 81% of targets are taken before the NY open. The PM session is irrelevant. This is a London-session phenomenon.

4. Failures are consolidation, not misdirection

70% of failures are inside bars. The market doesn’t fake the wrong direction — it just doesn’t move. A volatility filter may eliminate many of these.

5. Open question: regime dependence

All accuracy figures are pooled across 5 years of varying market conditions. Whether the 79% rate holds in trending vs ranging markets is untested. This is the critical next investigation.
